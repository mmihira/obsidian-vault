# Exploration & Expansion

## Spatial Progress

- Crystal networks are arranged in **cells** on the map
- Crystal networks produce [[Sanctuary/Combat/Fog System|fog clouds]] which the player must destroy
- Crystal networks will restore clouds the player has destroyed
- As the player ventures out and explores, they will get boxed in — creating a sense of danger and risk
- When the player has destroyed the source of the fog, the entire network is unlocked, allowing full access to resources
- Some [[Sanctuary/Crystals/Crystal Power & Networks#Crystal Network Nexus Gate|crystal network central nexus]] can be tapped to power the player's crystal power network

## Crystal Network Cells

- The cells involve destroying clouds which drop resources the player can harvest
- Dust/shard ratio controls how quickly the player can acquire shards to grow crystals
- [[Sanctuary/Combat/Tower System|Towers]] can either destroy clouds (high power cost) or simply push clouds back (lower power cost)
- Aggressive clouds detect towers close to the center and fight back by thrusting clouds at the player

## Within Cells

- Actual crystals which the player can harvest if they can get close enough
- These crystals are responsible for growing [[Sanctuary/Crystals/Crystal Types & Structures#Crystal Catalyst|crystal catalyst]] which the player needs to grow crystals
- Later, the player can build crystal catalyst harvesters for automatic collection

## Tower Upgrades

- Player can upgrade towers to make them more efficient

## Key Exploration Spell

**Fog Repulsor** — the player can place it anywhere; good for exploring the map to find different network variants. The spell can be overwhelmed by aggressive clouds.

## Endgame Exploration

- After the player starts generating enough power, monsters spawn at the map outskirts and come for the town
- Becomes a massive [[Sanctuary/Combat/Tower System|tower defense]] game requiring towers at specific locations
- Beating progressively powerful waves requires red gem tech
- Fog attack vs monster attack scale differently

## Slow Progression Systems

- Need a slow level progress system built into the world like dungeons with levels
- Dungeon levels represent macro progress over long horizons
- Need map features the player can continuously attack or devote resources to upgrade (similar to Stardew Valley's mine)
